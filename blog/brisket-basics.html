The Brisket Basics

A simple, repeatable brisket workflow: pick the right cut, season well, go low-and-slow, be patient through the stall, then rest and slice.
Home Blog

If you only remember three things: 225°F and steady, don’t panic during the stall, and rest before slicing.

Picking the brisket

  • Look for a well-marbled brisket (USDA Prime or Choice), ideally with a thicker fat cap.
  • Brisket is two muscles (flat + point), which is why low-and-slow works so well.

Trim + season

Trim

  • Trim excess fat and silverskin, leaving enough fat to protect during the smoke.
  • Keep it simple: clean shape, no huge hard fat chunks.

Sol’s Special Dry Rub

Mix in a bowl, then coat generously.
  • 2 tbsp kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp coarse black pepper
  • 1 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)

Smoke (low and slow)

  • Set smoker to ~225°F (107°C) and hold it steady.
  • Use a smoking wood you like (hickory, oak, or mesquite are classic choices).
  • Smoke until you approach the stall; focus on consistency more than the clock.

Practical note: if you want to add your “house style” later, we can standardize spritzing, wrap timing, and slice thickness into a repeatable checklist.

The stall (where patience wins)

  • The stall happens when internal temp plateaus. Don’t rush it.
  • When you’re ready, wrap in butcher paper or foil (“Texas crutch”) to retain moisture and push through.

Finish + rest

  • Finish when internal temp is around 200°F (93°C) and the probe feel is tender.
  • Rest at least 30 minutes before slicing so juices redistribute.

Serve

  • Serve simply and let the brisket speak for itself.
  • Optional sides: barbecue sauce, pickles, and white bread (classic Texas-style).
