Italian Chicken Roulade

Chicken rolled with soppressata, capicola, and provolone—finished with a roasted tomato yogurt sauce (tzatziki-adjacent, no cucumber).
Chicken Italian Weeknight+
Home Food Projects
Italian chicken roulade, sliced. This is one of those dishes that eats even better the next day.

Method

  1. Prep the chicken. Butterfly chicken breasts (or use thin cutlets). Cover with plastic wrap and pound to an even thickness.
  2. Season. Salt, pepper, garlic powder, and Italian seasoning. Optional: a thin smear of Dijon helps everything bind.
  3. Build the roulade. Layer soppressata, capicola, and provolone. Leave a small border so it rolls cleanly.
  4. Roll + secure. Roll tight like a jelly roll. Tie with butcher’s twine every 1–1.5 inches (toothpicks work in a pinch).
  5. Sear. Hot pan + neutral oil. Brown all sides for color and flavor.
  6. Finish cooking. Transfer to a 375°F oven until the center hits 155–160°F, then rest. (Or keep in the pan, reduce heat, cover, and cook gently.)
  7. Rest + slice. Rest 10 minutes. Slice thick (½–¾”) and spoon sauce over the top.
As you re-cook this, we’ll lock in exact amounts and times and convert this into a precise, repeatable recipe card.

Ingredients

  • Chicken breast (butterflied) or chicken cutlets
  • Soppressata
  • Capicola
  • Provolone
  • Salt, pepper, garlic powder, Italian seasoning
  • Neutral oil for searing
  • Optional: Dijon mustard

Roasted tomato yogurt sauce

  • Plain yogurt (you used Fage 0%)
  • Roasted tomatoes (or blistered cherry tomatoes)
  • Lemon (juice + zest)
  • Garlic (microplaned)
  • Olive oil, salt, pepper
  • Optional herbs: dill or parsley

Notes
