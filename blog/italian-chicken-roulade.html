The story

The first time I made this, my dog had just had surgery and was wearing the cone. He wasn’t eating well, so I made him boiled chicken. He wanted none of it. But this roulade? Suddenly he was a food critic—he’d only eat it if I fed him by hand.

Why this one is worth it

  • It looks fancy. It’s basically “rolled chicken,” but it plates like a restaurant.
  • It’s forgiving. Sear for flavor, then finish gently so it stays juicy.
  • The sauce makes it. Cold, tangy sauce over warm sliced roulade is a power move.
Make-ahead note: You can assemble the roulade earlier in the day, then sear + finish when you’re ready.

Serving ideas

  • Sautéed vegetables (what you did) + the sauce
  • Roasted potatoes or a simple pasta if you want it more filling
  • Leftover slices make an elite sandwich

Recipe

Ingredients

  • Chicken breast (butterflied) or chicken cutlets
  • Soppressata
  • Capicola
  • Provolone
  • Salt + pepper
  • Garlic powder + Italian seasoning
  • Neutral oil (for searing)
  • Optional: Dijon (thin smear helps everything stick)

Roasted tomato yogurt sauce

  • Plain yogurt (you used Fage 0%)
  • Roasted tomatoes (or blistered cherry tomatoes)
  • Lemon (juice + zest)
  • Garlic (microplaned)
  • Olive oil, salt, pepper
  • Optional herbs: dill or parsley

Instructions

  1. Prep the chicken. Pound to even thickness (plastic wrap on top helps).
  2. Season. Salt, pepper, garlic powder, Italian seasoning. Optional: thin Dijon smear.
  3. Fill. Soppressata + capicola + provolone, leaving a small border for clean rolling.
  4. Roll + secure. Roll tight. Tie with butcher’s twine every 1–1.5 inches (or toothpicks).
  5. Sear. Hot pan + oil. Brown all sides.
  6. Finish gently. 375°F oven until cooked through, then rest. (Or cover the pan and cook low.)
  7. Rest + slice. Rest 10 minutes. Slice thick. Sauce over top.
Slice tip: Use a sharp knife and commit. Hesitation makes the cheese sad.
