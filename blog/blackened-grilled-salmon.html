The whole trick is heat + timing. The sauce is the victory lap.

Why I love this one

Salmon is one of the rare proteins that can feel “restaurant” on a random Tuesday. The blackened seasoning gives you instant swagger, and the dill yogurt sauce keeps everything bright. It’s salty, smoky, tangy, and just a little extra.

Why it works

  • Dry fish browns better. Pat it dry and you’ll actually get crust instead of steamed sadness.
  • Skin-on is forgiving. It protects the flesh and helps you nail doneness.
  • Cold sauce, hot fish. The contrast makes it feel intentional, not improvised.

Ingredients

Exact measurements will evolve as you re-cook it. These are the “don’t overthink it” basics.

For the salmon

  • Salmon fillets (skin-on recommended)
  • Oil (avocado oil is great for high heat; olive oil works too)
  • Blackening seasoning (or DIY: paprika + garlic powder + cayenne + salt + pepper)
  • Lemon (for finishing)

For the dill yogurt sauce

  • Plain yogurt (Greek-style works well)
  • Fresh dill (or parsley if dill is missing-in-action)
  • Lemon juice + a little zest
  • Garlic (microplaned)
  • Salt + pepper
Optional upgrade: if you want it richer, do half yogurt / half mayo.

How to make it

  • Preheat: medium-high grill, clean and oil the grates.
  • Season: oil the salmon lightly, then apply blackening spice generously.
  • Cook: skin-side down first; flip only when it releases easily.
  • Rest: 2–3 minutes, then sauce and serve.

Serving ideas

  • Rice pilaf + grilled asparagus (classic “I planned this” plate)
  • A simple green salad if you want lighter
  • Leftovers: flake into a salad or wrap (cold salmon is underrated)

Storage

Best within 1–2 days. Store sauce separately. Reheat salmon gently (low oven), or eat it cold in a salad so you don’t overcook it on the second pass.

Recipe (quick version)

  1. Prep. Pat salmon dry. Lightly oil. Season generously with blackening spice.
  2. Preheat grill. Medium-high. Clean grates and oil them well.
  3. Grill. Skin-side down first. Cook until it releases; flip briefly to finish (or don’t flip if thin).
  4. Rest. 2–3 minutes.
  5. Sauce + serve. Spoon on dill yogurt sauce. Finish with lemon.
Next iteration: we’ll add “what to look for” doneness cues and your exact spice blend.

Dill yogurt sauce (quick)

Mix yogurt + lemon + microplaned garlic + chopped dill + salt/pepper. Thin with a splash of water or olive oil if needed.
