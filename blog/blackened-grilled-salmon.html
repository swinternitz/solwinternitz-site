Why I keep making this

Salmon is one of the rare proteins that can feel “restaurant” on a random Tuesday—as long as you respect heat and timing. Blackening seasoning brings instant swagger, and the dill yogurt sauce makes the whole plate feel composed instead of “I grilled a fish and hoped for the best.”

Why it works

  • Dry fish browns better. Patting it dry is the difference between crust and steamed regret.
  • Skin-on is forgiving. It protects the flesh and makes the cook window wider.
  • Cold sauce + hot fish. Contrast = instant “this was planned.”

My “don’t mess this up” tips

  • Oil the grates. Fish sticks when you skip this. Ask me how I know.
  • Don’t force the flip. When it’s ready, it releases. When it’s not, it tears.
  • Stop cooking at almost-done. Rest finishes the job.

Ingredients + swaps

For the salmon

  • Salmon fillets (skin-on recommended)
  • Oil (avocado oil is great for high heat; olive oil is fine)
  • Blackening seasoning (or DIY: paprika + garlic powder + cayenne + salt + pepper)
  • Lemon (finish with juice + a little zest if you’re feeling fancy)

For the dill yogurt sauce

  • Plain yogurt (Greek-style is ideal)
  • Fresh dill (parsley works if dill is missing-in-action)
  • Lemon juice + zest
  • Garlic (microplaned)
  • Salt + pepper
Optional upgrade: if you want it richer, do half yogurt / half mayo.

How to make it

  • Preheat: medium-high grill; clean and oil the grates.
  • Season: oil salmon lightly, then apply blackening spice generously.
  • Cook: skin-side down first; flip only when it releases easily.
  • Rest: 2–3 minutes.
  • Sauce: spoon on dill yogurt sauce and hit it with lemon.

Equipment notes

  • Tongs + a fish spatula if you have it (not required, but helpful)
  • Instant-read thermometer (optional, but makes you feel invincible)

Serving ideas

  • Rice pilaf + grilled asparagus (classic “I planned this” plate)
  • Green salad + roasted potatoes if you want more substance
  • Leftovers: flake into a salad or wrap (cold salmon is underrated)

FAQ + storage

How do I know it’s done?

The salmon should flake easily and look opaque, but still juicy. If you use a thermometer, aim for “just done,” then rest. (We’ll add your preferred target temp once you tell me how you like it.)

Can I do this without a grill?

Yes. Cast iron works: sear, then finish in the oven. Same seasoning, same sauce, same attitude.

Storage

Best within 1–2 days. Store sauce separately. Reheat salmon gently (low oven), or eat it cold so you don’t overcook it on the second pass.

Recipe card (quick version)

  1. Prep. Pat salmon dry. Lightly oil. Season generously with blackening spice.
  2. Preheat grill. Medium-high. Clean grates and oil them well.
  3. Grill. Skin-side down first. Cook until it releases; flip briefly to finish (or don’t flip if thin).
  4. Rest. 2–3 minutes.
  5. Sauce + serve. Spoon on dill yogurt sauce. Finish with lemon.

Dill yogurt sauce (quick)

Mix yogurt + lemon + microplaned garlic + chopped dill + salt/pepper. Thin with a splash of water or olive oil if needed.
