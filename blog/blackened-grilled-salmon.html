Blackened Grilled Salmon Feast

High heat, short cook, rest briefly, then sauce. Don’t overthink it.

Why I keep making this

Salmon is one of the few proteins that can feel “restaurant” with almost no effort—as long as you respect heat and timing. The blackened seasoning gives you a punchy crust, and the dill yogurt sauce keeps it bright and balanced.

Why it works

  • Dry fish + hot grill = better browning. Patting dry matters more than people think.
  • Skin-on is forgiving. It protects the flesh and buys you a little extra time.
  • Cold sauce, hot fish. The contrast makes it feel composed, not thrown together.

Tips (the stuff that prevents sadness)

  • Oil the grates, not the fish. I still do both, but grates first is the “no-stick” lever.
  • Don’t force the flip. When it’s ready, it releases. When it’s not, it tears.
  • Stop cooking at “almost done.” Rest finishes the job.

Variations

  • No dill? Parsley + lemon still works.
  • Want richer? Do half yogurt / half mayo.
  • No grill? Cast iron works: sear, then finish in oven.

Storage

Leftovers keep 1–2 days. Reheat gently (low oven) or eat cold in a salad. The sauce keeps separately and gets better after a few hours.

Recipe

  1. Prep. Pat salmon dry. Lightly oil, then season generously with blackening spice (or paprika + garlic + cayenne + salt + pepper).
  2. Preheat grill. Medium-high. Clean grates and oil them well.
  3. Grill. Skin-side down first. Close lid. Cook until it releases easily, then flip briefly to finish (or don’t flip if thin).
  4. Rest. 2–3 minutes.
  5. Sauce + sides. Spoon on dill yogurt sauce. Serve with rice pilaf and grilled asparagus.

Ingredients

  • Salmon fillets (skin-on recommended)
  • Oil (avocado or olive)
  • Blackening seasoning (or paprika + garlic + cayenne + salt + pepper)
  • Plain yogurt (or mayo/yogurt blend)
  • Fresh dill
  • Lemon (juice + zest)
  • Garlic (microplaned)
  • Rice pilaf + asparagus (to serve)

Dill yogurt sauce

Mix yogurt + lemon + garlic + chopped dill + salt/pepper. Thin with a splash of water or olive oil if needed.
If you want it richer, do half yogurt / half mayo.
